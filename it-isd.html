What is Instructional Systems Design?

Instructional Systems Design (ISD) is the systematic process for determining any learning disparities and developing an intervention or instruction to improve the effectiveness and efficiency of the learning process. Throughout the process, instructional designers determine the needs of the learner, end goal of instruction, and expected learning outcomes, all of which guide the development of the instruction or intervention. The instruction is built upon the best practices from various learning theories.

About ISD Models

There are numerous ISD models available for use by instructional designers today. The most common model is the ADDIE model which was developed in the 1970s by Flordia State University’s Center for Educational Technology as part of a project for the U.S. Military. ADDIE stands for Analyze, Design, Develop, Implement, And Evaluate. There are various other models; however they are rooted in the ADDIE Model. Some of the other models commonly used by instructional designers today include:

  • The Dick & Carey Model
  • Four-Component Instructional Design (4C/ID) model by Van Merriënboer
  • Holistic 4D model by Charles M. Reigeluth & Yunjo An
  • Backward Design model by Wiggins & McTighe
