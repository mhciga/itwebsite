Meet Our Legends!

Important People in IT Field

Burrhus Frederic Skinner (B.F. Skinner) was a psychologist best known for developing the theory of behaviorism and Operant Conditioning. Additionally, he created what is known as the “Skinner box” which was conditioning apparatus he used to study behavior and learning based on how animals interacted with their environment. During his career, he published 21 books and over 180 articles.

B.F. Skinner

+

Robert Gangé was an American educational psychologist. He is most known for his book titled Conditions of Learning which outlines nine steps for planning instruction and nine events of instruction. Additionally, he is known for his stimulus-response theory of eight kinds of learning. He made significant contributions to the cognitive and constructive learning theories. His work had a profound influence on education, military and industrial training.

Robert M. Gangé

+

Jean Piaget was a psychologist best known for his contributions to the theory of constructivism. His research concentrated on developmental psychology and genetic epistemology with the goal of understanding how does knowledge grow. Additionally, he defined four stages of childhood cognitive development. During his career, he published 50 books and 500 articles/papers including 37 volumes in the series “Etudes d’Epistémologie Génétique” (Studies in Genetic Epistemology).

Jean Piaget

+

Robert Reiser is currently the associate Dean for Research and a Distinguished Teaching Professor of Instructional Systems at Florida State University. He has published 5 books and more than 75 articles related to the field of IT. He is best known for his book Trends and Issues in Instructional Design and Technology which is in its 4th edition. His research interests include evaluating web-based and computer-based instruction, instructional planning practices of teachers, and instructional design competencies.

Robert Reiser

+

Charles Reigeluth is an education theorist whose research focuses on paradigm change in public education, the design of high-quality instruction, and the design of technology systems for the learner-centered paradigm of education. He is most known for his four-volume book titled Instructional-Design Theories and Models, which are referred to as the “four green books” within the field of IT. He and Dr. Yunjo An published the Holistic 4D Model for instructional design which greatly improves the effectiveness, efficiency, and appeal of the ID process.

Charles Reigeluth

+

David Merrill is considered one of the founding fathers of the instructional design field. He is best known for the development of the Component Display Theory, Elaboration Theory, and Instructional Transaction Theory. He is considered a pioneer in the development of computer-based instruction. In addition to his theories, he developed an instructional authoring system known as TICCIT, Time-shared Interactive Computer Controlled Information Television. He has published 12 books and over 193 journal articles.

David Merrill
