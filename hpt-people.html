Meet Our Legends!

Important People in HPT Field

Thomas Gilbert was a psychologist who is considered the father of performance technology, now known as Human Performance Technology (HPT). He used his knowledge of behavioral psychology to improve human performance at school and work. He coined the term “Performance Engineering” and developed the Behavior Engineering Model. He is best known for his book titled "Human Competence: Engineering Worthy Performance."

Thomas Gilbert

+

Joe Harless was instrumental in the transformation of the Human Performance Technology field. He is the father of the front-end analysis, a critical first step in determining the influences on a performance gap. He was a protegé of Thomas Gilbert and was one of the most successful pioneers of systematic and systemic performance improvement. In addition to the Front End Analysis, Joe created the Performance Chain model.

Joe Harless

+

Robert Mager was an educational psychologist who helped transform the Human Performance Technology Field. He is one of the founders of the International Society for Performance Improvement. He developed the Criterion Referenced Instruction Model. The framework of the model is based upon using objectives to drive instruction; the objectives need three components, performance, condition, and criterion. During his career, he published 10 books. His book Preparing Instructional Objectives is the best-selling book written on the subject.

Robert Mager

+

Geary A. Rummler was a pioneer in the application of instructional and performance technologies to organizations. He founded and directed the University of Michigan’s Center for Programmed Learning for Business. He developed and taught world-class methodology through performance improvement workshops. Additionally, he defined five components of a performance system. He published numerous books and is best known for his book "Improving Performance: How to Manage the White Space on the Organization Chart."

RGeary A. Rummler

+

Dale Brethower is a Professor Emeritus, Psychology, at Western Michigan University. He is best known for his book Performance-based Instruction. He challenges Performance Technologists to focus on the job when developing instruction to link training to business results. He has more than 77 publications to his name.

Dale Brethower

+

Allison Rossett is a recently retired Professor of Educational Technology from San Diego State University. She has made major contributions in improving workplace learning and performance. She has published six books and continues to consult on learning strategy, instructional design and technology, and performance support.

Allison Rossett
