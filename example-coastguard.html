HPT & IST in the Coast Guard

Instructional Systems Technology (IST) and Human Performance Technology (HPT) are both prevalent in various aspects within the Coast Guard. IST is rooted in the military stemming from the necessity to rapidly and effectively train recruits during WWII. The Coast Guard has the Force Readiness Command (FORCECOM) which is dedicated to ensuring the Coast Guard has a highly trained, professional, and ready workforce. FORCECOM has multiple divisions which oversee training, exercises, assessments, and career investment. Additionally, the Coast Guard has 14 training centers and 16 assessment units that fall under the supervision of FORCECOM.

The 14 training centers are located throughout the U.S. and host a variety of different training courses, from 16-week technical job trainings to 1-week courses, all taught through in-person instruction. IST professionals located at each of the training centers manage the development, implementation, and evaluation of courses. They ensure that the courses are teaching the required skills and knowledge need for members to effectively perform their assigned duties and complete the Coast Guard’s missions. Course content is continually being updated to address the changes in technology implemented at field units as well as the changes in tactics and procedures utilized by units given the dynamic nature of operations.

The Coast Guard uses a learning management system to administer online courses to personnel. These courses range from 30 minutes to hour-long yearly mandated training courses to law enforcement courses designed to facilitate the learning of fundamental knowledge. IST personnel manage the development, implementation, and evaluation of these courses. Another area where IST personnel use their skills is in the development and evaluation of job-specific qualification requirements, guides, and procedures. Most job-specific learning occurs through on-the-job training. Members work through a qualification package under the guidance of qualified peers or senior members. These qualification packages have defined knowledge and skill checks used to assess the unqualified member to ensure that the member has gained the required knowledge and proficiently demonstrate the essential skills. As new platforms, tools and technology are introduced to the Coast Guard, IST personnel use summative feedback to evaluate the effectiveness of the qualification packages and update them to ensure the packages effectively facilitate learning the new knowledge and skills required to operate the new platform, tools or technology.

In addition to the training centers, the Coast Guard has a Performance Technology Center that specializes in using HPT models to address systemic performance gaps within the Coast Guard. Members at this center work with various programs within the Coast Guard to address performance issues. They conduct front-end analysis to determine the root cause, develop and design interventions, and provide the program with a list of recommended interventions to eliminate the performance gap. Once the interventions have been implemented, specialists evaluate their effectiveness and make recommendations to the program regarding any changes required.

These are just a few examples of IST and HPT within the Coast Guard. If you wish to learn more, please check out the Coast Guard Force Readiness Command Website
