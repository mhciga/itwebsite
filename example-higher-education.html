IST in Higher Education

Compared to primary and secondary education, which focus on systematic instruction and standardized assessment, it has been only about two decades since higher education introduced Instructional Systems Technology (IST) and Human Performance Technology (HPT) into the campus. To improve IST in higher education, most colleges and universities now have a certain institution such as the Center for Teaching and Learning Development, the Teaching Center, or the Center for Innovative Teaching and Learning, which are engaged in the development of IST and provide the faculty as well as the staff with multiple instructional services. For HPT’s part, both schools and the government emphasize the importance of higher education evaluation as a way to market the university. To further demonstrate the practical implementation of IST and HPT in higher education, let’s use higher education in Taiwan as an example.

National Taiwan University, one of the top higher education schools in Taiwan, officially set up the “Center for Teaching and Learning Development (CTLD)” in 2006, in which the team of “Faculty Development” is based on one of the different task objectives. The CTLD has been engaged in promoting IST for the faculty and students who are enthusiastic to share teaching experiences to seek the enhancement of teaching quality. According to the website, it is responsible for holding new faculty members’ camps, organizing Faculty Empowerment Series Activities, training teaching assistants, offering learning consultation and academic affairs planning & research, etc. The IST programs held by the center include:

  • Faculty Development Program

    • The Faculty Development Program teaches faculty members how to proceed with Inquiry-Based Learning. Through the process of discovering problems, forming hypotheses, proving hypotheses, and reaching conclusions, the faculty members can encourage their students to engage in active deep learning, and subsequently cultivate interest and generate a sense of accomplishment.

    Img: Faculty Development Program (NTU CTLD)

  • T+ Camp

    • Seminars and interactive activities are organized to create opportunities for cross-disciplinary exchanges among faculty members. This program tries to build a ‘recharge station’ in faculty members’ career path in the hope of assisting faculty members to examine their own teaching philosophy and self-worth through intensive training and exchange in teaching knowledge & skills so as to have themselves ‘recharged’ for the journey ahead and open up a new era for their teaching.

    Img: T+ Camp (NTU CTLD)

  • Faculty Plus - Tips for Digital Teaching

    • In response to the COVID-19 pandemic, the “educational technology application ability” has become an important issue for the professional growth of teachers today. NTU CTLD recently launched “Faculty Plus - Tips for Digital Teaching.” Through a series of video and physical courses, faculty members can learn about techniques of creating digital teaching materials and have an understanding of new teaching modes step by step. The CTLD continues to incorporate the most innovative IST for its faculty members and students at National Taiwan University.

    Img: Faculty Plus (NTU CTLD)

    HPT in Higher Education

    For HPT’s part, the Higher Education Evaluation and Accreditation Council of Taiwan (HEEACT) was founded in May 2005. The goal of the HEEACT is “To promote the development of every university, the Ministry of Education shall organize an Assessment Committee or commission academic organizations or professional accreditation bodies to carry out regular assessments of the universities” (HEEACT Website). With the aims to improve the quality of Taiwan higher education, HEEACT provides the following services in accordance with the relevant regulations:

  • It supports the government and HEIs in promoting the quality assurance in higher education accreditation and evaluation.
  • It conducts higher education accreditation and research with local and foreign institutes.
  • It promotes cooperation and exchanges with overseas quality assurance agencies and international networks.
  • It supports the government plan and implements various higher education projects and initiatives.
  • It conducts activities related to public good and educational affairs that are aligned to HEEACT mission.

    • HEEACT has completed two rounds (2006 - 2010 & 2012 - 2016) of the evaluation, completing the evaluation of at least 79 schools (1907 departments). The evaluation indicators are as follows:
